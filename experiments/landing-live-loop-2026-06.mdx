## Why this hypothesis
The ICP is the pre-PMF founder who ships daily with agents — they don't need another feature list, they need proof that fast shipping can compound into learning. The bet, decided with the founder: the **conversion moment is just seeing** Systemix's own loop running live — real experiments rendered as their AI workflows — not closing a loop themselves. The live dogfood *is* the pitch, and it doubles as the lead magnet for consultancy.

## Given
A pre-PMF founder lands on Systemix with a half-formed idea and an agent-heavy stack.

## The AI workflow
The loop the founder sees running, dogfooded: a raw idea → Hermes frames the hypothesis (ICP · JTBD · metric) → build the variant → measure (PostHog) → a human decides (HITL) → the learning is written back. The diagram above is this experiment's own workflow, rendered from its frontmatter — the experiment about the loop *is* a loop.

## Conclusion
Founders who see the live, dogfooded loop book a call at a meaningfully higher rate than those who only get the feature pitch. Signal wiring (the metric → a PostHog event) is pending — connecting a data source is a separate setup step — so this runs as a registered bet until evidence lands.

## Decision criteria
- `promote`: the live-loop variant lifts book-a-call meaningfully over the feature pitch → make it the landing.
- `iterate`: positive but short → tighten what the founder sees first (which experiment, how much loop).
- `kill`: no lift → seeing the loop isn't the hook; the magnet is something else (revisit the ICP or the pitch).
