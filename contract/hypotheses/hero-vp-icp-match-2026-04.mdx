## Why This Hypothesis

The current hero copy leads with token drift and design-code parity — a designer-first message. Feedback from early users (ops directors, founding engineers) indicates the primary unlock is the evidence-feedback loop: building something, shipping it, and getting PostHog data written back into the design rationale automatically. Token sync is a feature; hypothesis validation is the product.

## Variants

**Control** (`control`): "Design systems shouldn't lie. Systemix keeps them honest."
Positions Systemix as a correctness/parity tool. Primary emotion: frustration with drift.

**Variant B** (`variant_b`): "Ship faster. Measure everything. Let Hermes close the loop."
Positions Systemix as an agentic workflow accelerator. Primary emotion: shipping velocity.

## Success Criteria

- Primary: trial signup conversion rate (unique sessions → `/docs/quick-install` click)
- Secondary: time-on-page on the landing hero section
- Threshold: ≥20% lift on primary metric in Variant B to declare winner

## Decision Criteria

- `promote`: Variant B wins → update `src/app/page.tsx` hero copy and mark control deprecated
- `iterate`: lift &lt;20% but direction is positive → test a third variant with stronger CTA
- `kill`: no lift or negative → revert to control, investigate messaging with user interviews
