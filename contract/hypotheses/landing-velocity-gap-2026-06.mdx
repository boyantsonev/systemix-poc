## Why This Hypothesis

The prior hero (`landing-hero-icp-pivot-2026-05`) framed the pain as memory loss — "you made a decision three months ago and can't find why." That frame assumes a slow cadence. The product reality changed: Hermes can analyze and run the loop **daily**, not quarterly. At daily cadence the felt pain is no longer embarrassment over forgotten rationale — it is the **velocity gap**: shipping is 10× faster with agents, but learning is still manual and quarter-behind, so speed doesn't compound.

The control frames a single experiment closing. Variant B frames the founder's whole cadence: you ship daily, your learning should too. It positions Systemix as the layer that makes fast shipping actually compound.

## Variants

**Control** (`control`): "You shipped a hypothesis. Now close the loop."
Single-experiment, memory framing. Emotion: closure on one thing.

**Variant B** (`variant_b`): "You ship every day. Your system should learn every day too."
Cadence/compounding framing. Emotion: leverage — fast shipping that finally compounds.
