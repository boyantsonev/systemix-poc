## Why This Hypothesis

The current use-cases section addresses "product teams" and "teams already running PostHog." Both cards imply an existing team, existing tooling, existing process. The pre-PMF founder ICP has none of that. They have Claude Code, a Vercel deploy, a PostHog account, and a gut feeling about what to build next.

The founder's job is not "close the loop your analytics never closed" (team framing). It is "don't make the next product decision from memory." That is a solo job, done in the 20 minutes between shipping and sleeping. The current copy does not speak to that moment.

Variant B collapses the two generic team cards into one high-specificity founder card that mirrors the actual decision cadence: you shipped something → you need to know if it worked → before you ship the next one. The loop closes in a single sentence.

## Variants

**Control** (`control`): Two team-audience cards with "product teams" and "PostHog teams" framing. Generic use-case grid.

**Variant B** (`variant_b`): Single founder-first scenario card with first-person framing. Followed by a smaller "teams" card as a secondary use case. Founder comes first.
