## Why This Hypothesis

The current HITL preview card has a critical accessibility and conversion problem: text opacity is set to 30–60% (`text-muted-foreground/30`, `/40`, `/60`), making it nearly unreadable against the white background. The "Promote variant" button is styled as a ghost with no visual weight — it does not read as a primary action. The card fails WCAG AA contrast requirements throughout.

The hypothesis is not about aesthetics. It is about signal clarity: the founder is looking at the product's primary UX surface. If they can't read the card clearly, they do not trust the product. A well-lit, high-contrast card with a clear primary action communicates: "this is real software, not a mockup."

Glassmorphism with a dark section also creates a strong visual break on the landing page — the card becomes a product demo moment rather than an illustration. This pattern (light → dark → light) is proven to increase dwell time on the feature section.

## Variants

**Control** (`control`): Current flat card on white background. Low contrast text throughout. Ghost-style buttons. No visual hierarchy between Promote / Run longer / Discard actions.

**Variant B** (`variant_b`): Dark section (`bg-zinc-950`) with glassmorphism card (`backdrop-blur-xl`, `bg-zinc-900/70`, `border-zinc-700/50`, deep shadow). Full contrast text. Filled primary button (emerald, with glow shadow). Clear secondary and tertiary button styles. Emerald blob background for proper glassmorphism depth.

## Success Criteria

- Primary: clicks on "Open the dashboard →" link below the card (`hitl_cta_click` PostHog event)
- Secondary: scroll depth past the HITL section (founder reads all the way through)
- Accessibility gate: card passes WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text) — non-negotiable regardless of conversion result
- Threshold: ≥20% lift on primary metric, or accessibility gate alone justifies shipping

## Decision Criteria

- `promote`: Variant B wins or accessibility gate alone — ship the dark section card, update all landing page docs
- `iterate`: Neutral result → test intermediate option (light section, frosted card, improved contrast only — no dark section)
- `kill`: Dark section reduces scroll-past rate — revert to light section, but still fix the contrast issues in control
