**Done means:** a client instance wears the client's brand through token
overrides alone — primary color, font, radius — with zero component forks,
client stakeholders sign off, and a perception check reads "feels like our
product" from at least four of five reviewers.

**Kill if:** the theming turns out to need component-level forks. That breaks
the engine's promise that it wears your brand rather than imposing one — and it
must be discovered before more clients onboard, not after.

## Why this goal

Systemix is an embedded, per-client engine. The shell and every prototype it
renders must match the client's look and feel, or the "your repo, your brand,
your contract" story fails on sight. The style-match skill exists for exactly
this; applying it through the queue is the validation.

## State of play

**Parked** — no client design-system instance is attached yet (Connecta is
partner-one candidate). The skill is ready; this goal activates when a client
instance does.
