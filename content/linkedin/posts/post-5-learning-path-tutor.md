## Content pack

### 📋 Post brief
- **Core idea:** The same learning loop shown in Track A — evidence written back to a decision — applied to a learner instead of a token. Connecta Research: a customizable learning path that updates itself from signals about the learner. Framed as open research: mechanism demonstrated, outcomes unproven, questions explicit.
- **Format:** Native video walkthrough (<90s, square 1:1 or 4:5, burned-in subtitles) + text post. Link in first comment, never in body.
- **Goal:** Surface WHICH audience exists for learning-loops-as-pedagogy, and make 2–3 senior practitioners (educators, adaptive-learning builders, or skeptics) reply or DM.
- **Target:** Instructional designers, edtech builders, ML/agent engineers who think about eval loops, design-systems people who followed Track A.
- **Best time:** Tue–Thu, 8–10am audience-local. Reply to every comment in the first hour. No edits in the first 24h.

### 🧭 Narrative
Four posts in, the loop is established on machines: a decision ships, evidence flows back, the decision corrects itself. Post 5 asks the uncomfortable version of the question — does the loop hold when the substrate is a person? Connecta Research is a learning-path tutor where the path is the decision, the learner's behavior is the evidence, and the write-back rewrites what comes next. The honest core: the mechanism works on screen, but whether adaptation actually helps learning — or just feels responsive — is the open question, and the K-12 grounding (under-18 users, hard privacy walls) raises the stakes on getting it right.

**OPTION C — Pattern 7: The Direct Question** *(edu-native framing — use if upstream replies came from learning/education people)*

```
Why does a learning path stay frozen after the learner starts walking it?

Courses adapt once, at the start — a placement test, maybe a preferences quiz — then go static for weeks while the most useful evidence piles up unused: where the learner slows down, what they retry, what they quietly skip.

I've spent four posts showing design systems that write evidence back to decisions. Connecta Research is me testing whether the same loop holds when the substrate is a person.

The walkthrough (first comment, under 90 seconds, works muted) shows a self-updating path: a learner struggles with one concept, and the next three steps reorganize around it. Not a recommendation engine — a plan that revises itself and shows its reasoning.

Two caveats, because this is research in public, not a launch:

1. The related K-12 case has under-18 users. Privacy is a hard constraint — everything in the demo is synthetic. No real learners, no real names, no exceptions.

2. I can show the mechanism. I can't yet show it improves outcomes. That's the open question, and I'd rather say so than imply otherwise.

Educators, AI folks, designers — I suspect you'll each see a different flaw in this. I want all three.

#EdTech #LearningDesign #BuildingInPublic #AIinEducation
```
